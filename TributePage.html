Nikola Tesla

"Let the future tell the truth and evaluate each one according to his work and accomplishments. The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine."

Timeline of the life of Nikola Tesla:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, in the Austrian Empire (current day Croatia).
  • 1861 - Attended primary school in Smiljan where he learned German, arithmetic, and religion.
  • 1862 - His whole family moved to Gospić, Croatia, where he finished primary school and middle school.
  • 1870 - Moved to Karlovac, Croatia to attend high school at the High Real Gymnasium. Here, he picked up an interest in electricity due to the demonstrations of his physics teacher.
  • 1873 - Graduated within 3 years of what was supposed to be a 4-year term. Prior to graduating, his teachers believed that he was cheating as he had the ability to perform integral calculus in his head. He returned to Smiljan after graduation where he contacted cholera, and almost died multiple times.
  • 1875 - After hiding and wandering the mountains to avoid conscription, he attended the Imperial-Royal Technical College in Graz on a Military Frontier Scholarship. He garnered praised and admiration due to his capabilities but failed upon reaching his third year, as to what was attributed to failure to focus on his studies due to distractions.
  • 1879 - Convinced by his father to return to Gospić but was desported to due not having a residence permit. His father died the month after that at the age of 60 due to an illness. He spent the rest of the year teaching a large class of students in his old school.
  • 1880 - Despite the financial help of relatives, he was unable to enroll to the Charles-Ferdinand University in Prague as he arrived too late, and was illiterate in Greek and Czech, both being required subjects. He attended lectures in Philosophy as an auditor in stead.
  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest, Hungary to work at the Budapest Telephone Exchange. During his employment, he had made many improvements to the Central Station equipment, and claimed to have perfected a telephone repeater or amplifier, which was never patented or publicly described.
  • 1882 - Began working at Edison where he gained pratical experience in Electrical Engineering, and having his work recognized, he was sent to other Edison Utilities around France and Germany to troubleshoot engineering problems.
  • 1884 - Emigrated to the United States, working at the Machine Works on Manhattan's Lower East Side. This is also the time where he first met Thomas Edison, whom unknown to him at the time would be his biggest rival. He later quit due to rumored unpaid bonuses which is one of the sources for the infamous but unproven Edison quote "Tesla, you don't understand our American humor", pertaining to his bonuses only being a joke.
  • 1885 - In pursuit to obtaining some patents, he met with an attorney that introduced him to 2 businessmen that agreed to finance a company in his name, the Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing Company. He tried to obtain many patents including an improved DC generator, which was commended on its advanced features.
  • 1886 - A time of hardship for Tesla as he was left penniless due to abandonment by the company's investors and lost of his patents.
  • 1887 - With 2 other men, they formed the Tesla Electric Company, agreeing to divide profits equally. Here, Tesla developed an induction motor that ran on Alternating Current (AC) which was highly recognized in Europe and the US. The patent of the motor, though would cause some problems for Tesla and his partners, would still make him quite wealthy years down the line.
  • 1889 - Having the funds to now be free to pursue his interests, Nikola Tesla was able to come up with the Tesla Coil with the help of external inspirations. The development of the Tesla coil would later be very useful for his wireless power work.
  • 1891 - Became a naturalized citizen of the United States. The same year the Tesla coil was patented.
  • 1892 - Served as vice-president of the American Institute of Electrical Engineers until 1894, the forerunner of the modern-day IEEE, along with the Institute of Radio Engineers.
  • 1893 - At an institute, he discussed the eventual possibility of Wireless Lightning by conducting it through the earth. He also patented the Steam-powered oscillating generator at the same year.
  • 1895 - The Nikola Tesla Company was founded with the assistance of his previous colleagues which handled Tesla's patents for the decades to come. The same year, the building that hosted his lab caught fire which not only set back his current projects but destroyed collection of his early notes, research material, models, and demonstration pieces.
  • 1896 - With his experimentation of X-ray imaging, he developed a circuit that could improve the orindary apparatus of his time.
  • 1898 - Demonstrated a radio-remote-controlled boat which he intended to sell to the US Military as a type of radio-controlled torpedo, but they showed little interest. Remote radio control remained a novelty until World War I and afterward, when a number of countries used it in military programs.
  • 1901 - Guglielmo Marconi successfully transmitted the letter "S" from England to Newfoundland, defeating Tesla in a race to be the first to complete such a long-distance transmission, which was the focus of Tesla for a few years now. Tesla moved on to instead focus on an even larger plan to transmit messages by controlling "vibrations throughout the globe", thus began his downfall as he lost funding, faced financial issues, and suspectedly went through a nervous breakdown.
  • 1906 - Demonstrated a bladeless turbine which found its use in luxury car speedometers and other instruments.
  • 1916 - Received AIEE Edison Medal.
  • 1926 - Received Grand Cross of the Order of St. Sava
  • 1931 - Received Cross of the Order of the Yugoslav Crown. On this same year, a young journalist whom he befriended organized a celebration for his 75 birthday party where he received congratulations from respective figures in the field of science such as Albert Einstein. It went so well that it became an annual event.
  • 1932 - Claimed to have invented a motor that would run on cosmic rays.
  • 1934 - Told reporters that he had designed a superweapon he claimed would end all wars called the "teleforce" or more usually referred to as his "death ray". He received the John Scott Medal on the same year. 1937 - Received Grand Cross of the Order of the White Lion and the Medal of the University of Paris. On this tragic year, the 81 year-old Nikola Tesla left his hotel to make his regular commute to the Cathedral and library to feed the pigeons. Upon crossing a street, he was unable to dodge a moving taxicab which resulted in several injuries. He refused to consult a doctor, an almost lifelong custom, and never fully recovered.
  • 1939 - Received the Medal of the University of St. Clement of Orchrida.
  • 1943 - At the age of 86, Tesla died alone in Room 3327 of the Hotel New Yorker. The medical examiner ruled the cause of death to be coronary thrombosis (a type of heart attack).
